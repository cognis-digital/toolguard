Toolguard is a security layer that controls which actions AI agents are allowed to take on your behalf. You define a policy — a list of permitted and forbidden tools, argument rules, and rate limits — and Toolguard checks every tool-call against it before it runs, blocking or sanitizing anything that violates your rules. It is useful for developers building AI-powered applications who need to prevent their agents from accidentally (or maliciously) accessing sensitive files, hitting internal systems, or leaking credentials. You can use it from the command line, wire it into your CI pipeline, or expose it as an MCP server that any compatible AI assistant can query in real time.
